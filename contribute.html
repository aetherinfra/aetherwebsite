Regarding Contribution

Monetary Contribution is completely optional. Your contributions in the form of code, documentation, design, or testing are what truly drive the project forward. However, if you choose to support us financially, it helps us maintain our build servers and ARMv8 test hardware. To contribute code, visit our GitHub repository and follow the contribution and rules listed below.
Note: For mobile users, the Github link is available inside the footer links section at the bottom of the page.

Contribution Workflow

  • Fork & Clone: Create a personal fork of the Aether WebOS repository and clone it to your local AArch64 development environment.
  • Feature Branch: Always create a new branch for your feature or bugfix (e.g., feat/virtio-net).
  • Atomic Commits: Ensure your commits are small, descriptive, and follow our naming conventions.
  • Pull Request: Submit your PR to the main branch. The Core Maintainers will review your code for architectural alignment.

Rules & Code of Conduct

To maintain a professional research environment, all contributors must adhere to:

  • Technical excellence and code safety.
  • Respectful communication in PR comments and Issues.
  • No plagiarism; all code must be original or appropriately licensed (GPL/MIT).
  • Maintain the "Bare Metal" philosophy—minimal dependencies.

Contributor Recognition

The Core Contributor Certificate is awarded to new contributors upon the successful merge of their first significant architectural PR.

  • Certificates are awarded based on architectural impact (as determined by the Core Maintainers).
  • Returning contributors are tracked via the Aether Badge System.
  • Milestone awards are available for long-term project commitment (10+ successful PRs).

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Do I need to be a Systems expert to contribute?

Not at all. While the core kernel is low-level C, we have massive needs for Web Developers (for the WebOS Management Portal), Technical Writers (for documentation), and UI/UX Designers. If you can code, there is a layer of the stack for you.

2. Is Aether an official MSIT college club?

No. Aether Infrastructure is a purely personal research project led by a group of students. It is not affiliated with, funded by, or governed by MSIT or any of its official clubs. We operate independently to maintain research flexibility.

3. How do I earn the Contributor Certificate?

Certificates are awarded for "Significant Architectural Impact." This means moving beyond typo fixes—think implementing a new driver, optimizing a syscall, or building a core feature for the WebUI. Once your PR is merged, the core-maintainers team will issue your digital credential.

4. I'm into Cybersecurity—is there anything for me?

Yes. A core part of Aether is Privilege Isolation and Memory Safety. We need people to help audit our AArch64 exception levels (EL1/EL0) and stress-test our custom network stack for vulnerabilities.

5. Can I contribute if I don't have an ARMv8 machine?

Absolutely. Most of our development and testing happens via QEMU emulation. As long as you have a standard laptop/PC, you can simulate the entire AArch64 environment locally to test your code.

Privacy & Security

Contributor Data

We only collect your name and email for the purpose of issuing contribution certificates. Your data will never be sold or shared with third parties.

Vulnerability Reporting

If you discover a security flaw in Aether WebOS, please report it via private message to the Core Maintainers rather than opening a public issue immediately.
